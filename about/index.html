I’m a research assistant on the Global Health & Development team at Rethink Priorities, an effective altruist research organization. I also copyedit Asterisk, a quarterly magazine about existential risk. And I donate 10% of my income to the most effective charities. (If any of this sounds unfamiliar but interesting, maybe check out 80,000 Hours, an Oxford-based organization that offers free career advising.)

I recently graduated from the University of Chicago, where I majored in environmental science. Environmental science is housed in the geosciences department at UChicago, so I got to do cool stuff like research lead pollution and do a thesis on the orbital history of Mars. My transcript includes quite a bit of history, some computer science, and a sprinkling of classical Chinese, in addition to the Core Curriculum. I chose America for college because I wanted a liberal arts education.

In 1999 I was born in London, England, to Chinese academics. We moved (back) to China in 2007, where I lived until age 18. We lived in Chengdu, a cloudy city in the South West known for its pandas and peppercorns. Journalism occupied most of my time in high school; I founded and edited a bilingual student magazine for three years and briefly dreamed of being a magazine editor when I grew up.

As of 2022 I live in London, again, with my partner Puyu, who’s doing a master’s at University College London and who took the photo of me at Loch Ness. I like national parks, board games, maps, and most things that are sufficiently spicy. I recommend listening to Robert Wright’s Nonzero (formerly The Wright Show) and Very Bad Wizards.
