As a research assistant, I review literature, analyse data, and write reports on population health, international development, and climate change policies and interventions at Rethink Priorities, an effective altruist research organization. I also copyedit Asterisk, a quarterly magazine about existential risk. And I donate 10% of my income to the most effective charities.

I grew up in London and Chengdu, in southwestern China. I set off for America for college because I wanted a liberal arts education. From 2018 to 2022, I attended the University of Chicago, where I majored in environmental science. Environmental science is housed in the geosciences department at UChicago, so I got to do cool stuff like research lead pollution and do a thesis on the orbital history of Mars. My transcript includes quite a bit of history, some computer science, and a sprinkling of classical Chinese, in addition to the Core Curriculum.

As of 2022 I live in London, again, with my partner Puyu, who’s doing a master’s at University College London and who took the photo of me at Loch Ness. I like national parks, board games, maps, and most things that are sufficiently spicy. I recommend listening to Robert Wright’s Nonzero (formerly The Wright Show), Very Bad Wizards, and The 80,000 Hours Podcast with Rob Wiblin.
