I’m starting a new job as a research assistant on the Global Health & Development team at Rethink Priorities, an effective altruist research organization. I also copyedit Asterisk, a quarterly magazine about existential risk. I’ve also pledged to donate 10% of my income to the most effective charities for the rest of my life or until I retire. If any of this sounds unfamiliar but interesting, maybe check out 80,000 Hours.

I recently graduated from the University of Chicago, where I majored in environmental science. Environmental science is housed in the geosciences department at UChicago, so I got to do cool stuff like spend time in Woods Hole, Massachusetts, researching lead pollution and do an optional thesis on the orbital history of Mars. My transcript includes quite a bit of history, some computer science, and a sprinkling of classical Chinese, in addition to the Core Curriculum. I went to college in America because I felt it was important to get a liberal arts education.

In 1999, was born in London, England, to Chinese academics. My mum’s an academic psychiatrist who specializes in schizophrenia, and while I don’t really know what my dad researches I do know he’s a chemist-turned-physicist-turned-biologist. I was raised by my waipo or maternal grandma (1942–2022), who was a primary school teacher and had the best Sichuan chili oil recipe.

My family moved back to China in 2007, where I had most of my formative experiences. We lived in Chengdu, a cloudy city in the South West known for its pandas and peppercorns, where I went to international school. Journalism occupied most of my time in high school; I founded and edited a bilingual student magazine for three years and briefly dreamed of being a magazine editor when I grew up.

As of 2022, I live in London with my partner Puyu, who’s doing a master’s degree at University College London. I like national parks, board games, and spicy food. I use en-GB-oxendict (which means I write “analyse” but “synthesize”, prefer my en-dashes spaced – like so – and my commas “untucked”, and always use the serial comma) and can appreciate a good haiku.
