I’m starting a new job that involves reviewing literature, analysing data, and writing up reports on population health, international development, and climate change policies and interventions at Rethink Priorities, an effective altruist research organization. I also copyedit Asterisk, a quarterly magazine about existential risk. If any of this sounds unfamiliar but interesting, maybe check out 80,000 Hours.

I recently graduated from the University of Chicago, where I majored in environmental science. Environmental science is housed in the geosciences department at UChicago, so I got to do cool stuff like spend time in Woods Hole, Massachusetts, researching lead pollution and do an optional thesis on the history of Mars’s orbit. I also did some history, some computer science, and some classical Chinese, in addition to the Core Curriculum. I went to college in America because I felt it was important to get a liberal arts education.

In 1999, was born in London, England, to Chinese academics. My mum’s an academic psychiatrist who specializes in schizophrenia, and while I don’t really know what my dad researches I do know he’s a chemist-turned-physicist-turned-biologist. I was raised by my waipo or maternal grandma (1942–2022), who lived through Japanese air raids on Chungking, the Cultural Revolution, the post-Deng economic miracle, and Covid-19 lockdowns.

So much of my time was spent rewatching BBC science and nature documentaries, rereading Roald Dahl’s children’s corpus, and flipping forwards and backwards through a dilapidated copy of the Usborne Children’s Encyclopedia. My family moved back to China in 2007, where I had most of my formative experiences. We lived in Chengdu, a cloudy city with pandas and mouth-numbing peppercorns, where I went to international school. I was into journalism in high school, where I founded and edited a bilingual student magazine for three years and briefly considered being a magazine editor when I grew up.

As of 2022, I live in London with my partner Puyu, who’s doing a master’s degree at UCL. I like national parks, board games, and spicy food. I use en-GB-oxendict (which means I write “analyse” but “synthesize”, prefer my en-dashes spaced – like so – and my commas “untucked”, and always use the serial comma) and can appreciate a good haiku. I’ve also pledged to donate 10% of my income to the most effective charities for the rest of my life or until I retire.
